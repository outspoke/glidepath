Information Collection and Use

Glidepath collects information that allows us to provide you with a full range of customized services. The information that we collect from our users includes the following:

INFORMATION THAT YOU SUBMIT

Glidepath collects and retains any information that you submit to our website or provide to us in any other way, including information regarding your employment search (if you are a candidate) or your candidate search (if you are an employer).

  • Registration. In order to use certain Glidepath services, you must first complete a registration form that requires certain personal information including your name, business name (if applicable), and relevant contact information. You will also be required to create a user name and password for access to your account.
  • Settings and Preferences. Upon registration or any time you use your account, you will have the ability to adjust the profile, preferences and settings associated with your account. These settings may include, for example, the choice to receive email alerts or other communications from Glidepath and the choice to store information in your account, such as records of searches, communications or other account activity.
  • Orders. If you purchase a product or service from us, we request certain personally identifiable information from you. You must provide contact information (such as name, email, and shipping address) and financial information (such as payment card number and expiration date). We use this information for billing purposes and to fill your orders. If we have trouble processing an order, we will use this information to contact you. Glidepath will process payment card information according to rules of the applicable payment processor.
  • Applications. If you are a candidate and submit a resume, CV or other job application information to Glidepath, we will store such information within your account and our candidate database. GlidePath will share such information with employers to which you apply, as well as other employers that search Glidepath’s candidate database.
  • Questionnaires. Certain employers may request responses from candidates to customized surveys or questionnaires. If you provide such responses, Glidepath will share them with the applicable employer. If you choose not to provide such responses, an employer may not accept your job application.
  • Communications. We will send you a welcoming email to verify your user name and password. You may choose to receive newsletters or email alerts from Glidepath about new features and services, special deals or promotions, or (if you are a candidate) alerts about new jobs that match your preferences, application history, location or other information associated with your account. In the future, we may also offer the choice to receive such information by mobile messaging.
  • Other Correspondence. We will also communicate with you in response to your inquiries (including customer support inquiries), to provide the services you request, and to manage your account. In such correspondence, you may choose to provide your email address, name, comments or other personal information.

In addition to the particular uses of information described above, Glidepath uses your submitted information as described below:

  • Glidepath uses submitted information to provide you with our products and services, including the employment search and candidate search and management. We also use such information for auditing, research and analysis; maintaining, protecting and improving our services; developing new services; and ensuring the proper functioning of our systems and infrastructure.
  • The Services may combine the information you submit under your Glidepath account with information from third parties (including employers) in order to provide users with a better experience and to enhance our services. For example, employers may submit to their Glidepath accounts application or other information from third party sources. Glidepath may also enable employers to obtain such third-party information through the service user interface, for example, by requesting a background check on applicants from a Glidepath business partner, subject to any required consents.
  • Glidepath processes personal information on our servers in the United States of America. In some cases, that may mean that we process personal information on a server outside your own country.

INFORMATION THAT WE COLLECT AUTOMATICALLY

Glidepath and its authorized agents automatically collect and retain certain types of information whenever you interact with Glidepath’s website and services.

  • Log files. As is true of most Web sites, we gather certain information automatically through users’ user of the Website, and we store it in log files. This information includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, operating system, date/time stamp, and clickstream data. We use this information to maintain your User Profile (as described below) and to analyze trends, to track users’ movements around the site, to gather demographic information about our user base as a whole, and to administer and improve the Website and our services.
  • Cookies. Cookies are small text files stored on a user’s computer for record-keeping purposes. Glidepath uses both session ID cookies and persistent cookies to enable our systems to maintain your login status, recognize your browser, store your password (if desired), and record how and when our website and services are utilized. Most Web browsers allow users to receive notification when new cookies are set, specify which cookies to accept and reject, or disable cookies entirely (consult your browser’s “Help” menu for guidance on adjusting your settings). We recommend that you accept all cookies from Glidepath because turning them off may prevent or impair your access to our website and services.
  • Clear Gifs. We may also employ a software technology called clear gifs (a.k.a. Web Beacons/Web Bugs), that help us better manage content on our site by informing us what content is effective. Clear gifs are tiny graphics with a unique identifier, similar in function to cookies, and are used to track the online movements of Web users. In contrast to cookies, which are stored on a user’s computer hard drive, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on Web pages and are about the size of the period at the end of this sentence. We may also use clear gifs in our HTML-based emails to let us know which emails have been opened by recipients. This allows us to gauge the effectiveness of certain communications and the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.
  • User Profile. We store information that we collect through cookies, log files, clear gifs, to create a “User Profile” of your preferences. We tie this information to the information in the profile and account in order to provide more relevant content and services to you.

Children

Glidepath does not knowingly collect personal information about children under the age of 13. If we learn that GlidePath has received information from a child under the age of 13, we will delete that information as quickly as possible. We recommend that minors between the ages of 13 and 17 ask and receive their parents’ permission before using Glidepath or sending information about themselves or anyone else over the Internet.

Security

Glidepath has implemented procedural and technological measures intended to secure your personal information from accidental loss and from unauthorized access, use or disclosure. However, Glidepath cannot guarantee that unauthorized persons will not be able to defeat those measures or use your personal information improperly. You are responsible for the security of your Glidepath username and password. We strongly recommend that you never share your Glidepath login information with others and that you protect yourself against unauthorized access to your account by logging off once you have finished using any computer that is accessible to others. For your convenience, Glidepath allows you to manage your email alert settings and preferences (such as search keywords and location) via links included in emails that we send to your inbox, and do not require a username or password to view alert information or change settings. If you share a GlidePath email alert, the recipient will be able to access your alert settings and preferences.

How to Control Your Information

  • You can choose not to disclose information to Glidepath, although doing so may limit your ability to use our website and services.
  • You can modify your preferences regarding communications from Glidepath, either by adjusting the settings in your account or by following the instructions contained in any unwanted communications. However, please note that you may still receive administrative communications (such as welcome emails upon registration and responses to forgotten password requests) and legal notices from us as long as you remain a registered user of our website. Generally, you may not opt-out of these communications, which are not promotional in nature. If you do not wish to receive them, you have the option of deactivating your account.
  • You can control how your browser handles cookies from GlidePath, although disabling cookies from Glidepath may limit or impair your access to our website and services.
  • You can update your information at any time on our website. However, note that when you do so we may maintain a copy of the unrevised information in our records.
  • You can request deletion of your GlidePath account by contacting us at support@glidepath.com.

Protect Yourself from Scams

Glidepath uses automated processes to collect and present information that has been created by third parties, so we cannot assure the accuracy or legitimacy of that information. We strongly recommend that you exercise caution when providing information about yourself to others online, including when you seek or contact candidates or employers through Glidepath. To protect yourself, we suggest the following:

  • Never provide social security number, credit card information, bank account details, or unnecessary personal or financial information to a potential employer or a candidate.
  • Never participate in a financial transaction of any sort with another user of the Website.
  • Always be cautious of opportunities that sound too good to be true or require you to act as a financial intermediary, particularly for an overseas person.
